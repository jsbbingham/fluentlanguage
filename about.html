About Isela Bingham

Professional Spanish Interpreter & Document Translator

20+ Years of Professional Experience

I am Isela Bingham, a Spanish Bilingual Interpreter and Translator based in Stockton, California, with over 20 years of professional experience. I am deeply committed to serving individuals, families, and organizations across legal, medical, and educational settings. My work is incredibly rewarding, and I find immense fulfillment in leveraging my skills to support the Spanish-speaking community and witness the positive impact on families.

Professional Background

Since 2006, I have provided independent contracting services for various state and county-based agencies, including Valley Mountain Regional Center, the San Joaquin County Office of Education (Transworld Stockton), Now Interpreters, Excel Interpreting, and Family Resource Network. I also offer services to individuals for court cases. My expertise includes translating complex documents such as medical reports, psychological evaluations, Individualized Family Service Plans (IFSPs), occupational reports, physical therapist reports, and Individual Program Plans (IPPs). I provide both in-person and Video Remote Interpreting (VRI) services for numerous counties. Additionally, I spent two years supporting Linden Elementary, Waverly Elementary, and Glenwood Elementary Schools.

I have extensive experience in simultaneous interpretation, including large-scale conferences with over 100 attendees, as well as consecutive and sight interpretation in both face-to-face and VRI environments. My background includes interpreting for local and surrounding county School Board Meetings, assisting with phone calls, mailing resources, and in-person interpretation for families at the Families Resource Network. During the COVID-19 shutdown, I facilitated access to training by delivering tablets to families. I have also interpreted for VMRC Board Meetings, various committee and subcommittee meetings, and special grant meetings, such as the Partners In Policy-Making Conference for VMRC from 2022 to 2023. In 2015, I worked at the Down Syndrome Symposium for DDS with Dena Hernandez at UOP, focusing on best practices in educating children with Down Syndrome. Recently, I completed in-person interpreting for ASL training for San Joaquin Families in 2026. Furthermore, I frequently interpret for psychologists conducting autism and immigration psychological evaluations, and I assist with training alongside an immigration expert to emphasize the importance of proficiency in interpretation for accurate evaluation findings.

Professional Environments

My career has exposed me to a wide array of professional environments. I have supported attorneys in workers' compensation law and served as an Assistant Clients' Rights Advocate, where I attended trainings with attorneys to assess psychological evaluations and advocate for families to receive fair services when denied. I also participated in Social Security phone calls where attorneys appealed denials for families. I was an Outreach Spanish Speaker for the Modesto Office for OCRA and also conducted trainings as to what OCRA is and services that are provided for the community. My training includes conducting Advance Health Care Directives with adult consumers to empower families in making informed medical decisions. I regularly interpret for School Individualized Education Program (IEP) meetings, Autism Evaluations, Board Meetings, and Court Interpretations. This diverse experience has provided me with a deep command of legal, specialized needs, and institutional terminology in both English and Spanish, not only verbally but also in document translation, both in person and remotely.

Current Contracting

I currently contract with Reliable Translations, Inc. and Now Interpreters, accepting both remote and on-site assignments, including IEP meetings at schools throughout the Stockton area. I actively support Valley Mountain Regional Center across various needs, offering both in-person and remote assistance for IPPs, psychological evaluations, intake assessments, and ongoing support for interactions with teachers, speech therapists, occupational therapists, and physical therapists.
